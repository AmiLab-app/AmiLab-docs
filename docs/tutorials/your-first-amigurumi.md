# Your First Amigurumi

**Time:** ~10 minutes  
**What you'll make:** A sphere shape with a complete, ready-to-use crochet pattern.

By the end of this tutorial, AmiLab will have calculated every round for you — no graph paper, no formulas, no spreadsheets. You'll see firsthand why designers call it "the math that does itself."

---

## Before you begin

You need:
- A browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Safari)
- The AmiLab web app open in a tab

No account required to follow along.

---

## Step 1: Create a new project

On the home screen, click **Create New**.

A dialog asks for a project name. Type something like `My First Sphere` and click **Create**.

[Insert Screenshot: "Create New" dialog with a project name filled in]

AmiLab opens the editor with a blank canvas.

---

## Step 2: Select the Sphere shape

In the left panel, find the **Shape** section and click the shape picker icon. A grid of shape options appears.

Select **Sphere**.

[Insert Screenshot: Shape picker with "Sphere" highlighted]

The profile editor and 3D preview both update immediately. You should see a round silhouette on the left and a rough 3D shape on the right.

---

## Step 3: Switch to Realistic render mode

In the top-right corner of the 3D preview, click the **Render** toggle and select **Realistic**.

[Insert Screenshot: Render toggle showing "Realistic" selected]

The preview now shows the sphere as if it were made of actual crochet stitches — rows of texture wrapping around the surface. This is the shape your finished piece will have.

---

## Step 4: View your pattern

Click the **Pattern** tab in the right panel.

[Insert Screenshot: Pattern tab showing round-by-round instructions]

You'll see a full round-by-round stitch count, starting from the magic ring:

```
Round 1:  6 sc in magic ring  (6)
Round 2:  6 inc  (12)
Round 3:  [sc, inc] × 6  (18)
...
```

To copy the pattern to your clipboard, click **Copy Pattern**.

---

## What just happened?

When you selected **Sphere**, AmiLab used the shape's geometry to calculate exactly how many stitches are needed in each round for the fabric to lie flat and form a round surface. Change the radius, and the pattern updates. Change the gauge, and the stitch counts adjust to match your yarn. This is what "auto-math" means in practice — the app owns the calculations so you can focus on the design.
