# Copy your pattern as written instructions

AmiLab writes out your pattern as text as you design it — the round-by-round instructions a crocheter follows. Use this when you want to print the pattern, save it elsewhere, or send it to someone.

## Copy the text

1. Open the pattern in the editor.
2. Below the stitch grid, find the **written-pattern panel** — the row-by-row text version of your piece.
3. Click the **Copy pattern text** button (the copy icon) at the top of that panel.
4. Paste into your destination — a document, notes app, or message.

The text is copied to your clipboard. AmiLab regenerates it automatically whenever you edit, so what you copy always matches the current state of the piece — there is nothing to refresh or re-export.

## Notes

- The written pattern reflects your stitches, increases, decreases, and any [closure](./close-up-a-piece.md) you have applied.
- Stitch counts come from the pattern itself; the physical size depends on your [gauge](../explanations/gauge-and-dimensions.md).
