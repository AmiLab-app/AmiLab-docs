# Share a composition and its preview

When a [composition](./combine-pieces-into-a-composition.md) is ready, you can publish it as a public link. Anyone with the link sees an interactive 3D view of your character, your name as its maker, and the written patterns — no account required. Sharing is for compositions; individual patterns are shared as part of one.

## How sharing works

Sharing has two parts:

- **Public link** — turning a composition public creates a stable link you can copy and send. Anyone with it can open the viewer.
- **Preview image** — a snapshot of your composition that becomes the link's thumbnail and social-media card. AmiLab captures it from the current angle of the 3D preview, so you control how your piece is framed.

## Publish and copy a link

1. Open the composition in the **Compositions** view.
2. In the toolbar, click **Share**.
3. In the **Share composition** dialog, turn on **Public**.
4. The **Share link** appears. Click **Copy link**.
5. Click **Done** to save your sharing settings.

Send the copied link to anyone. To stop sharing, reopen the dialog and turn **Public** off.

## Set the preview image

AmiLab captures a preview automatically the first time you share. To choose the framing yourself:

1. Before opening Share, rotate and zoom the composition in the preview to the angle you want.
2. Open the **Share** dialog and click **Retake current view**.
3. The preview updates to match the canvas.

If you want to re-frame after the dialog is open, close it, adjust the canvas, reopen Share, and **Retake** — the capture always matches the current view.

## Get credited as the maker

The viewer shows "by *your name*" and links to your public profile — but only once your profile has a name. If the share dialog shows a reminder to **Complete profile**, click it to add your display name and picture (also available from your public profile page).

## What people see

Opening your link shows a viewer with:

- An **interactive, realistic 3D view** of the composition they can rotate and zoom.
- The composition's **name** and **your maker attribution**.
- A collapsible **pattern panel** with the full written instructions for every distinct piece — which they can copy. Mirrored pieces appear with their instructions reversed.

No sign-in is needed to view a shared composition.
