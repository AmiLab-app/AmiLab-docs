# Crochet Notation

AmiLab displays stitch abbreviations in your chosen language. The table below shows how the same stitch is written across all supported locales.

## Stitch abbreviations

| Stitch | en-US | en-GB | de | nl | ja | pt | zh |
|--------|-------|-------|----|----|----|----|-----|
| Single crochet | sc | dc | fm | lm | 細編 | pb | 短针 |
| Half double crochet | hdc | htr | hStb | hStk | 中長編 | mtp | 中长针 |
| Double crochet | dc | tr | Stb | Stk | 長編 | pa | 长针 |
| Treble crochet | tr | dtr | DStb | DStk | 長々編 | pd | 长长针 |
| Chain | ch | ch | LM | lo | 鎖編 | corr | 锁针 |
| Increase | inc | inc | Zun | toename | 増し目 | aum | 加针 |

## Notes on regional notation

### US vs UK English

US and UK crochet use the **same stitch names for different stitches**, which is a common source of confusion:

| US name | UK name | Same stitch? |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Single crochet (sc) | Double crochet (dc) | Yes — same stitch |
| Double crochet (dc) | Treble (tr) | Yes — same stitch |
| Treble (tr) | Double treble (dtr) | Yes — same stitch |

AmiLab stores stitches internally using US codes and translates them to your locale for display.

### German (Deutsch)

German amigurumi uses the standard Deutsche Häkel notation:
- **fm** = feste Masche (US sc)
- **Stb** = Stäbchen (US dc)
- **LM** = Luftmasche (US ch)
- **Zun** / **Abn** = Zunahme / Abnahme (increase / decrease)

### Japanese (日本語)

Japanese notation uses kanji compound names derived from the visual form of the stitch.

## Changing your language

Open **Settings → Language** in the app to switch notation. The pattern output updates immediately.
