# Pattern Output

Once your shape is designed, AmiLab generates a human-readable crochet pattern you can follow at your hook.

## Reading the pattern

Each line is one round, written in your chosen stitch notation:

```
Round 1:  6 sc in magic ring  (6)
Round 2:  6 inc  (12)
Round 3:  [sc, inc] × 6  (18)
Round 4:  [2 sc, inc] × 6  (24)
...
Round 12: 6 dec  (6)
Fasten off, leaving a tail for sewing.
```

The number in parentheses at the end of each line is the **total stitch count** for that round — use it to check your work.

## Exporting the pattern

You can:
- **Copy to clipboard** — paste into a notes app or document
- **Print** — use your browser's print function

## Language

The pattern uses the stitch abbreviations for your current language setting. Switch languages in **Settings → Language** and the output regenerates instantly.

See the [Crochet Notation](../crochet-notation.md) reference for a full list of abbreviations per locale.

## Gauge and sizing

If you set a gauge in **Settings → Gauge**, the pattern output includes approximate finished dimensions (height and width in cm) so you can plan your project.
