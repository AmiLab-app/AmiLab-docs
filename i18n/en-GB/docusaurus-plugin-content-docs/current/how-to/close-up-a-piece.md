# Finish off a completed piece

When a piece is shaped, its last round is still an open hole. **Finishing** tells AmiLab how that opening is closed, which updates both the 3D preview and the written instructions.

## Before you start

The **Finish** action is available once your piece has at least two rounds and the last round has at least two stitches, with the latest round active. If you don't see it, add a round or select the latest round first.

## Apply a finish

1. In the stitch controls bar, find the row actions and click **Finish**.
2. In the **Finish pattern** dialog, choose a method:
   - **Gathered close** — threads the yarn tail through all remaining stitches and pulls them tight. Best for spheres and rounded pieces.
   - **Folded seam** — folds the last round flat and seams the two edges together. Best for flat-bottomed pieces, pouches, and rectangular shapes.
3. For a **folded seam**, choose the stitch at the corner of the fold — the seam starts there and pairs up stitches from opposite sides.
4. Click **Apply**.

The Finish control now shows **Finished ✓**, and the preview and pattern text reflect the finished end.

## Change or remove a finish

Open the **Finish pattern** dialog again to switch methods, or click **Remove finish** to reopen the end.
