# Combine parts into a composition

A finished amigurumi is usually more than one part — a body, a head, arms, ears. A **composition** assembles your saved patterns into a single arrangement so you can see the whole character together and share it.

## Before you start

- Sign in — compositions are saved to your account.
- Have a few patterns saved in a **project**. A composition belongs to one project and draws its parts from that project, so make your patterns first (see [Getting Started](../tutorials/getting-started.md)) and keep them in the same project.

## 1. Open the Compositions view

1. Click the menu button (**☰**).
2. Under **Workspace**, click **Compositions**.

## 2. Start a composition

1. Click **New** in the toolbar.
2. In the **New composition** dialog, choose the **project** it belongs to and give it a name.
3. Click **Create**.

The composition belongs to that project, and the parts you can add come from it.

## 3. Add parts

1. Click **Add part**.
2. Pick a saved pattern from the list. It appears in the **Parts** panel and in the 3D preview.
3. Repeat for each part in your character.

Reuse the same pattern as many times as you need — two of one ear, four legs — by adding it more than once.

## 4. Position and orient each part

Click a part in the **Parts** panel to select it, then use the transform panel:

| Control | What it does | Range |
|---|---|---|
| **Position** X / Y / Z | Move the part in space (cm). | −50 to 50 |
| **Rotation** X / Y / Z | Turn the part (degrees). | −180 to 180 |
| **Mirror** | Flip the part — useful for making a left from a right. | on / off |

The preview updates live as you adjust. You can also toggle **Mirror** and remove a part directly from the **Parts** panel.

:::tip[Build symmetrical features as mirrors]

For matching pairs — arms, ears, legs — add the part twice, position the first, then add the second, position it on the opposite side, and turn on **Mirror**. The mirrored copy reads as the other side of the body, and its written instructions are reversed automatically when shared.

:::

## 5. Check the result

- Drag in the preview to rotate the whole arrangement.
- Click **Realistic** to see it in yarn-like colour, or **Schematic** for the structural view.

Your composition **saves automatically** to its project. Reopen or manage saved compositions from the **Files** dialog.
